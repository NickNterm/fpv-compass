# FPV Compass — Product Brief for Marketing

## One-liner
**The skill tree for FPV freestyle pilots — always know what trick to learn next.**

## Elevator pitch (30 sec)
FPV Compass is a curated dictionary and visual skill tree of freestyle drone tricks. Every trick links to the best YouTube tutorials on the internet, organized by difficulty and prerequisites — so pilots stop doom-scrolling YouTube and start progressing. Think of it as a tech tree for drone freestyle: complete one trick, unlock the next.

## The problem
FPV freestyle has a brutal learning curve and no structured path. Tutorials are scattered across hundreds of YouTube channels, skill levels are inconsistent, and pilots waste hours figuring out *what* to learn before they can even start learning *how*. Beginners plateau. Intermediates feel stuck. Everyone reinvents their own progression.

## The solution
One place that answers: **"What should I learn next?"**
- Every trick indexed with difficulty (1–10), pro tips, and prerequisites
- Curated links to the best tutorial for each trick — no fluff, no filler channels
- Progression visualised as a skill tree, not a wall of text
- Mark tricks as learned, see your path fill in

## Target audience
- **Primary:** FPV freestyle pilots, beginner to intermediate (6–24 months into the hobby)
- **Secondary:** Absolute beginners looking for a structured entry point
- **Tertiary:** Advanced pilots browsing/contributing community tricks

## Key features
- **Tree view** — visual skill map grouped by phase (Foundations → Basic → Intermediate → Advanced)
- **List view** — searchable, filterable flat list by difficulty
- **Progress tracking** — mark tricks as *learning* (orange) or *completed* (green)
- **Curated tutorials** — hand-picked YouTube links, not algorithmic slop
- **Community submissions** — pilots propose new tricks; admin-moderated into the canon
- **Reddit-style comments** — discussion and tips on every trick

## Differentiators
- **Not another video platform.** It's a map *to* the best content, not a competitor to YouTubers — which means creators benefit from traffic, not resent it.
- **Prerequisite-driven.** No other FPV resource models tricks as a dependency graph.
- **Opinionated curation.** Editorially chosen tutorials, not SEO noise.
- **Free, fast, no account needed to browse.**

## Brand personality
- Clean, technical, dark-mode dashboard aesthetic (think Linear or Vercel — not GoPro energy)
- Emerald accent = progress, orange = active learning
- Pilot-to-pilot tone: practical, no hype, respects the reader's time

## Future (phase 2+)
Expand beyond freestyle into cinematic, racing, and long-range. Becomes **the reference index for all drone flying disciplines** — a Wikipedia-meets-Duolingo for FPV.

## Launch hooks for marketer to explore
- Reddit: r/fpv, r/Multicopter (high-intent, very active)
- YouTube creator partnerships — tutorials get inbound traffic when linked
- Discord communities (IntoFPV, MultiGP, creator Discords)
- SEO goldmine: "how to learn power loop," "FPV tricks list," etc. — no dominant player yet

## Product facts (for reference)
- **Domain:** www.fpv-compass.xyz
- **Scope (MVP):** Freestyle tricks only; other disciplines in later phases
- **Auth:** Email/password; browsing works without an account
- **Content model:** Editorially curated + community submissions with admin moderation
- **Not:** A video host, a creator platform, or a course marketplace
